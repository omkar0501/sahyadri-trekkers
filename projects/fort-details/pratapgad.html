Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad Fort is a historic hill fort located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, near Satara. It is famous for the decisive battle fought here in 1659 between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan.

Historical Significance

Built by Shivaji Maharaj in 1656, Pratapgad was strategically important in defending the Maratha kingdom. The fort is renowned for the Battle of Pratapgad, where Shivaji Maharaj defeated the powerful Adil Shahi general Afzal Khan.

Trekking and Accessibility

The fort is accessible via a moderate trek from the nearby village of Par. Visitors can also reach it by road, and the trek offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Key Attractions

  • Statue of Shivaji Maharaj at the entrance
  • Pratapgad Battle Memorial
  • Old temples and bastions
  • Panoramic views of the Western Ghats

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Pratapgad Fort is from October to March when the weather is cool and pleasant.
