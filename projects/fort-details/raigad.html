Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort, perched atop the Sahyadri hills, served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is a symbol of Maratha pride and heritage.

Historical Significance

The fort was established as the Maratha capital in 1674 and is the site of Shivaji Maharaj’s coronation. Raigad was a strategic fortification known for its strong defenses and breathtaking views.

Trekking and Accessibility

Raigad Fort is accessible via a steep trek or a ropeway from the base village. The trek is moderately difficult but rewarding with scenic landscapes.

Key Attractions

  • Shivaji Maharaj’s samadhi (memorial)
  • The Maha Darwaja (main gate)
  • Ruins of palaces and watchtowers
  • Step wells and water cisterns

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Raigad Fort is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and the monsoon rains have rejuvenated the landscape.
