Purandar Fort

Purandar Fort is located about 50 km southeast of Pune and holds great historical and strategic significance in Maharashtra. This fort is well known as the birthplace of Sambhaji Maharaj, the son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Historical Significance

Purandar Fort played a major role during the Maratha era and was a stronghold of the Maratha Empire. In 1665, the Treaty of Purandar was signed here between Shivaji Maharaj and the Mughal general Jai Singh. It is also noted for being a military training base during the British rule.

Trekking and Accessibility

The fort can be reached by road, and there is also a moderately difficult trekking route starting from Narayanpur village. The trek is scenic, with a well-marked trail leading to the top, making it a rewarding experience for hikers.

Key Attractions

  • Kedareshwar Temple
  • Statue of Murarbaji Deshpande
  • Panoramic views of the surrounding Sahyadri hills
  • Ruins of old military buildings from British times

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Purandar Fort is during the monsoon and winter months, from June to February, when the landscape is lush and the weather is pleasant for trekking.
