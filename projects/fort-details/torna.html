Torna Fort (Prachandgad)

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandgad, is one of the highest forts in Maharashtra located in the Pune district. It holds a special place in Maratha history as the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1643 at the young age of 16.

Historical Significance

The fort played a vital role in Shivaji Maharaj’s early conquests and served as a strategic point due to its commanding height and natural defenses. It was later fortified further by Shivaji after capturing it from the Adil Shahi dynasty.

Trekking Experience

Trekking to Torna Fort is a thrilling experience for adventure seekers. The path is moderately difficult, with steep ascents and narrow ridges. Trekkers are rewarded with stunning views of the Sahyadri ranges and lush greenery, especially during the monsoon.

Key Attractions

  • Zunjar Machi and Budhla Machi – famous defense outposts
  • Temples of Mengai Devi and Toranjai Devi
  • Massive stone walls and bastions
  • Wide plateaus at the top for resting and exploring

Best Time to Visit

Monsoon and post-monsoon seasons (June to February) are best for visiting, offering cool weather, misty views, and blooming flora.
