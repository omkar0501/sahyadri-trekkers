Lal Mahal Pune

Lal Mahal, meaning "Red Palace," is a historic monument located in Pune, Maharashtra. It was originally built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's father, Shahaji Raje, in the 17th century, and served as an important center of Maratha power.

Historical Significance

Lal Mahal is famous as the place where Shivaji Maharaj spent his childhood. The palace played a key role in the Maratha resistance against the Mughal Empire. Though the original structure was destroyed, the current building is a reconstructed version preserving its legacy.

Visiting Information

Lal Mahal is located in the heart of Pune city and is easily accessible by road. Visitors can explore the palace grounds, the museum, and enjoy cultural exhibitions.

Key Attractions

  • Statue of Shivaji Maharaj
  • Reconstructed palace showcasing Maratha architecture
  • Exhibitions on Maratha history and culture
  • Beautiful gardens around the palace

Best Time to Visit

Lal Mahal can be visited year-round, with the best time being from October to February for pleasant weather.
