Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort, located around 30 km from Pune, is one of the most famous forts in Maharashtra. Perched atop the Sahyadri hills, it offers a blend of historical valor and breathtaking scenery.

Historical Significance

Sinhagad was originally known as Kondhana Fort. It gained legendary status after the fierce battle led by Tanaji Malusare, a general of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who captured the fort from the Mughals in 1670. Tanaji lost his life in the battle, leading Shivaji to say, "Gad ala, pan Sinha gela" (We gained the fort, but lost the lion).

Trekking and Accessibility

Sinhagad is accessible both by road and by a popular trekking trail that starts from the base village. The trek is of easy to moderate difficulty, making it ideal for beginners and families.

Key Attractions

  • Tanaji’s Memorial
  • Kalyan Darwaja and Pune Darwaza (main entrances)
  • Breathtaking panoramic views of the Khadakwasla Dam and Pune city
  • Local food stalls serving pithla-bhakri and curd

Best Time to Visit

Sinhagad can be visited throughout the year, but monsoon (June–September) and winter (October–February) are the most pleasant times to enjoy the greenery and cool climate.
