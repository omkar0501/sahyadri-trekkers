Raireshwar Fort

Raireshwar Fort is a scenic hill fort located in the Pune district of Maharashtra. It is famous for the ancient temple of Lord Shiva and its panoramic views.

Historical Significance

Raireshwar holds religious importance due to the 13th-century Raireshwar temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is also linked to the history of Shivaji Maharaj, as the fort is believed to be the site where he took his oath to establish the Maratha Empire.

Trekking and Accessibility

The fort is accessible by a moderate trek from nearby villages like Rajgurunagar. The trail passes through lush greenery and offers stunning views of the surrounding Sahyadri ranges.

Key Attractions

  • Raireshwar temple and its ancient idols
  • Scenic hilltop views
  • Shivaji Maharaj's legendary oath site
  • Water tanks and fort ruins

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Raireshwar Fort is between October and February when the weather is cool and pleasant.
