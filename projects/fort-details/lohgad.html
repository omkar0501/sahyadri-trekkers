Lohgad Fort

Lohgad Fort, situated about 52 km from Pune, is one of the most popular trekking destinations in the Sahyadri ranges. The fort’s name means "Iron Fort," reflecting its strength and strategic importance.

Historical Significance

Lohgad played a key role during the Maratha period and was used as a watchtower to guard the trade routes. It was captured by Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century and was an important part of the Maratha defense network.

Trekking and Accessibility

The fort is accessible via a moderate trekking route starting from the base village of Malavli. The path includes scenic views, rock-cut steps, and the famous Lohgad caves.

Key Attractions

  • Ganesh Gate – the main entrance
  • Historic water cisterns
  • Lohgad caves near the base
  • Views of the Pawna Reservoir and surrounding hills

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Lohgad Fort is between October and February, when the weather is cool and the landscape is lush green after monsoon rains.
