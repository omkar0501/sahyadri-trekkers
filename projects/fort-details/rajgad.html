Rajgad Fort

Rajgad Fort, located about 40 km southwest of Pune, served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj for nearly 25 years. It is known for its impressive hilltop location and rich historical legacy.

Historical Significance

Rajgad was originally called Murumdev Fort. It was renamed Rajgad, meaning "King's Fort," when Shivaji made it his capital. The fort witnessed many important events including the birth of Shivaji’s son Sambhaji and the planning of major military campaigns.

Trekking and Accessibility

Rajgad offers several trekking routes of varying difficulty, the most popular starting from the village of Pali. The trek passes through scenic forests and rocky ridges, rewarding visitors with panoramic views and historical ruins.

Key Attractions

  • Padmavati Temple
  • Raja Dindi (King’s residence area)
  • Rajgad Museum displaying artifacts and history
  • Ancient water tanks and bastions

Best Time to Visit

The best months to visit Rajgad Fort are during the cooler months from October to February and the monsoon season from June to September, when the surroundings are lush and green.
