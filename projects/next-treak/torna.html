Torna Fort Trek

Our Experience

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandgad, is a true test for those who crave adventure. It was one of the most thrilling treks we’ve undertaken – full of steep climbs, wind-swept plateaus, and history at every turn. The sense of achievement upon reaching the top is unmatched.

We started early morning, and the trail was alive with sounds of birds and fresh mountain air. The climb was challenging but extremely rewarding, especially once we reached the Balekilla and could see endless views of the Sahyadris. We also enjoyed exploring the fort's bastions and ancient water cisterns.

This is not a beginner's trek – it needs preparation and stamina. We recommend carrying light snacks, lots of water, and trekking poles if available. Reaching the top gives you a majestic 360° view and a peaceful silence that makes it all worthwhile.

About the Fort

Torna Fort is historically significant as it was the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Located around 70 km from Pune, it stands tall at over 1,400 meters and is one of the highest forts in the region.

Trip Duration

1 Day

How to Go (Travel)

Travel by bus from Pune. Pickup points:
• Swargate
• Katraj
• Navale Bridge

Food & Stay

Local villagers offer simple meals near the base. No formal stay arrangements at the top, but tents can be pitched with prior permission.

Things to Explore

  • Budhla Machi
  • Balekilla (Citadel)
  • Ancient Water Cisterns
  • Temple and Bastions

Trek Difficulty

This is a difficult trek suited for experienced trekkers. Steep ascents and long distance make it physically demanding.
